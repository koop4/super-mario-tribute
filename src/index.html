TIMELINE

  • 1985

  • Super Mario Bros

    The very first release, developed for the NES. This side-scrolling 2D platform started the brothers Mario and Luigi adventour in the Mushroom Kingdom.

  • 1986

  • Super Mario Bros

    The Lost Levels

    Sequel of the previous release, added complex levels, a final battle against Bowser, and a world with a lava-filled castel.

  • 1988

  • Super Mario Bros 2

    Based on a discarded prototype intended to become the first sequel. The main feature was the ability to pluck vegetables from the ground to throw at enemies.

  • Super Mario Bros 3

    Divided into eight themed world with 8-10 levels each, this game was the first in its series were levels could be played in a unlinear order. New powers allow Mario to jump higher, shoot, and even flight.

  • 1989

  • Super Mario Land

    The first handheld Super Mario title. Release for Game Boy

  • 1990

  • Super Mario World

    Release for the SNES consists of 9 worlds, and provides Mario a new spin jump and the ability to lift and throw items.

  • 1992

  • Super Mario Land

    6 Golden Coin

    Sequel of the hendheld console, introduces Wario, Mario's rival. The game story were continue in Wario Land

  • 1993

  • Super Mario All-Stars

    This was a collection release of the previous titles. Released for the SNES

  • 1994

  • Super Mario

    All Star + S.M. World

    Alternative version of the previous colection including Super Mario World

  • 1995

  • Super Mario World 2

    Yoshi's Island

    The protagonist, Yoshi, carries a Baby Mario with the goal to deliver him safely to the end of each level

  • 1996

  • Super Mario 64

    The first console 3d game, released for Nintendo 64, is an enclosed enviroment where the player is free to explore in all direction without time limits.

  • 1999

  • Super Mario Bros Deluxe

    Remake of the first game release for Game Boy Color.

  • 2001

  • Super Mario Advance

    Super Mario Bros 2 remake for Game Boy Advance.

  • Super Mario Advance 2

    Super Mario World remake for Game Boy Advance.

  • 2002

  • Super Mario Sunshine:

    Second 3D Super Mario title, released on the GameCube, with a gameplay similar to Super Mario 64. One of the most fashinating title ever.

  • Super Mario Advance 3

    Super Mario World 2 remake for Game Boy Advance.

  • 2003

  • Super Mario Advance 4

    Super Mario Bros 3 remake for Game Boy Advance.

  • 2006

  • New Super Mario Bros

    Released for Nintendo DS, has a 2D gameplay even if most characters are 3D object on a 2D background.

  • 2007

  • Super Mario Galaxy

    Wii adventure set in the outer space where Mario travels between galaxies. A mixin of a 3D and 2D game experience in which the player completely circumnavigate rounded planetoids.

  • 2009

  • New Super Mario Bros WII

    Remake of the onimous title, developed for the WII console, features a 4 players co-op and new power ups such as the propeller mushroom, the ice flower, and the penguin suit.

  • 2010

  • Super Mario Galaxy 2

    Sequel of the previous Galaxy title, retains the basic including new items and power ups. It also allow Mario to ride Yoshi.

  • Super Mario All-Start

    25th Anniversary

    Collection release of the Wii including an emulation of the original SNES ROM image which support the WII remote and classic controller.

  • 2011

    First handheld 3d release of Super Mario

  • 2012

    Sequel of the omonimous title, released for the Nintendo 3DS, in which the goal is also to collect million gold coins

  • New Super Mario Bros U

    Release for the WII console, introduce flying squirrel suits that lets the player glide through the air.

  • 2013

  • Super Mario 3D World

    The sequel to Super Mario 3D Land released for the Wii U introduced 3 new power ups: the super bell, lucky bell and double cherry.

  • 2015

  • Super Mario Maker

    A creation tool video game to allow players to create their own levels.

  • 2016

  • Super Mario Run

    A side-scrolling, auto-scrolling game released for iOS developed for mobile gaming.
